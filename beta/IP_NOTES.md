# ChainSight beta — IP / employment-contract checklist

Before you reach out to anyone tied to your current employer's customer base
or commercial network, run through this list. Don't skip it.

## What ChainSight is

- General-purpose AML risk scoring for **Ethereum-style wallets**
- Not specific to **gambling, casinos, sportsbooks, betting**, or **player
  account analysis** — those are the areas covered by your Bet365
  contract's invention-assignment clauses.
- Built on public regulatory frameworks (FATF, OFAC, EU/UK sanctions) and
  public chain data (Etherscan).
- Built on personal time, on personal hardware, with no employer property.

## What to avoid

- Do **not** reach out to anyone on the gambling-compliance side at Bet365
  itself, or at competitor sportsbooks where the work would clearly relate
  to your day job. Even if the product is generic, the optics are bad.
- Do **not** describe ChainSight as solving "betting customer risk
  analysis" or "player onboarding" use cases — that's the line your
  contract draws around what counts as employer IP.
- Do **not** demo ChainSight at a Bet365 work meeting, slack, or internal
  forum.

## What's safe

- Crypto exchanges, custodians, fund administrators, OTC desks, DAOs,
  lending protocols, accounting firms doing crypto attestation work,
  crypto compliance consultancies.
- Generic "AML compliance for crypto" content posted on your personal
  LinkedIn / Twitter — as long as it doesn't discuss employer-specific
  workflows or use cases.
- Open-source contributors and engineers in compliance fintech.

## If asked about your day job

Honest, brief, neutral:

> "I work in AML/CDD operations at a regulated business in Malta. ChainSight
> is something I'm building outside that role, focused specifically on
> crypto wallet screening, which isn't part of my employer's product."

Don't get drawn into specifics. If anyone presses, say you can't go into
detail and pivot back to the product.

## If you're unsure about a specific contact

Before sending any outreach to:
- A current or former Bet365 customer
- Anyone you met through Bet365 work
- Anyone at a sportsbook / casino / betting-adjacent company

→ Ask explicitly: "is this person a customer or commercial relationship
of my employer?" If yes, skip them.

## When to consider a formal carve-out

If you start signing real paying customers and the side-business stops
being theoretical, it's worth:

1. Reading your full Bet365 employment contract section by section,
   especially the assignment-of-inventions and non-compete clauses.
2. Talking to a Maltese employment lawyer (€200-400/hr; cheap insurance).
3. Considering whether to disclose to Bet365 in writing once the product
   has revenue, to put the question to bed.

You don't need to do this for the beta. You probably do need to before
charging your first customer.
