## Approach (2-week sprint)

**Week 1**
1. Pick 5 from Tier 1 — prioritise by warmest LinkedIn path.
2. For each, identify the named MLRO via LinkedIn + company AML reports. Don't email `info@`.
3. Send the OUTREACH.md "long" template, customised in 3 lines: name, regulator (MFSA / SEPBLAC / BaFin / DNB / etc.), and one specific recent compliance story they published.
4. Attach `AUDIT_AML_5_SPECIALISTS.md` as a credibility artefact — most vendors hide their gaps; you publishing yours is the differentiator.

**Week 2**
5. Follow-up #1 (4 days after no reply) — share the live `/methodology/coverage` link.
6. For any reply: book a 30-min call. Use the validation question above. Take notes verbatim — answers feed Option A scoping.
7. After 5 calls (or 10 days, whichever comes first), score: how many said "yes if blockers close"? **Threshold: ≥2 to proceed with Option A. <2 → reposition before building.**

---

## What "yes" looks like (decision criteria)

- 2+ MLROs explicitly say: "if those 8 blockers close, we'd pilot at €5k+/yr"
- 1+ explicit ask for a security questionnaire (real procurement signal)
- 1+ ask for SOC2 status / data residency

If any of those appear, **start Option A immediately** with rule-version pinning + audit log + sanctions sync as the first three items (because these are what each "yes" referenced).

## What "no" looks like (decision criteria)

- "We use Chainalysis, no need for a complementary tool" → ChainSight is too close to vendor parity claim. Reposition further (e.g., "open-source rule engine for internal teams" — different ICP).
- "We need primary screening, not decision-support" → wrong ICP; pivot to crypto-native fintechs (payments, B2B remittance) where there is no incumbent.
- Total silence after 10 cold + 5 warm outreach → the **pitch** is the problem, not the product. Rewrite OUTREACH.md.
